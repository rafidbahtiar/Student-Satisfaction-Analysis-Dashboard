# Student Satisfaction Analysis Dashboard
This project analyzes student satisfaction across various academic, financial, and demographic factors using Power BI. The goal is to identify key drivers of student satisfaction and provide actionable insights for improving the overall student experience in higher education institutions.

## Objectives
- Understand overall student satisfaction levels.
- Analyze the impact of teaching quality and campus facilities on satisfaction.
-	Evaluate how financial factors such as tuition and scholarships affect student experience.
-	Explore satisfaction differences across demographic groups.

# Dashboard Structure & Insight
## Page 1 - Overview
![page 1](page%201.png)

Key Insights:
- Most students come from a few major countries, indicating concentrated international enrollment.
- The majority of students do not receive scholarships, yet continue their studies.
- Online learning is widely adopted, reflecting a shift toward digital education.
-	Tuition distribution shows most students fall within a mid-range cost bracket.
-	Overall student satisfaction is generally positive.

## Page 2 - Teaching Quality & Campus Facilities Analysis
![page 2](Page%202.png)

Key Insights:
- Higher teaching quality is strongly associated with higher student satisfaction.
- Better campus facilities contribute to improved student experience.
-	Satisfaction varies across fields of study, indicating uneven academic experiences.
-	Some universities consistently achieve higher satisfaction levels.
-	Advanced program levels tend to show higher satisfaction.

## Page 3 - Financial & Access Factors
![page 3](page%203.png)

Key Insights:
-	Scholarship recipients generally report higher satisfaction levels.
-	Online and offline learning modes show comparable satisfaction outcomes.
-	Higher tuition does not necessarily guarantee higher satisfaction.
-	Satisfaction levels vary by year of study, suggesting changing experiences over time.

## Page 4 - Student Demographic Analysis
![page 4](page%204.png)

Key Insights:
-	Slight satisfaction differences exist across gender groups.
-	Certain age groups report higher satisfaction, especially mid-range age students.
-	Satisfaction levels vary by country, reflecting diverse educational experiences.
- Program level distribution shows a balanced student population.

## Conclusion
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of student satisfaction and its key influencing factors. The insights can support university administrators and policymakers in improving academic quality, financial accessibility, and overall student experience.
